## Archival Process

**During Weekly Review:**
1. Review all active Projects
2. Any stale >14 days? Archive or recommit
3. Archive Day Planners >7 days old
4. Note completion date if archiving completed project

**During Monthly Review:**
1. Review all Areas
2. Any neglected >60 days? Archive if no longer relevant
3. Review Resources for outdated material

**Naming Convention:**
`YYYY-MM-DD - Item Name`

Example: `2026-01-03 - Website Redesign Project`

## Your Chief of Staff Says

**Archive aggressively. Most people archive too late, not too early.**

If a project hasn't moved in 30 days, it's dead weight. Archive it. You can always restore if priorities change.

Your Archive folder has 505 files - that's GOOD. It means you've shipped things, completed work, made progress. An empty Archive means you're not executing.

**Founder anti-pattern:** Keeping zombie projects active "just in case." This creates decision debt.

If it's not moving the needle THIS month, archive it. Clear mental space for what matters now.

Your Day Planner Archive is your execution history. Don't delete these - they're proof of work and learning material for patterns.

**Archive liberally. Delete conservatively. Execute ruthlessly.**
