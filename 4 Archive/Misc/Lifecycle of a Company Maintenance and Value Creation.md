We are trying to build a business to capture value in the lifecycle of company formation to compliance. Expand all of these points in a similar style to the first point (Company formation) 

1. Company formation
	1. Decision points: LLC / Series LLC / C Corp?
		1. LLC / C Corp: LLC is a simpler structure and has passthrough tax benefits, whereas C Corp is double taxed
		2. Do you have office / employees in the US -- C Corp / LLC (8832 elect as corporation)
		3. Do you want to raise capital from VC / is it a startup with potential future investments: then C Corp
	2. Services
		1. Formation in DE (gold standard), WY (lower cost), NM (lowest cost to maintain and no yearly filing req)
		2. Paperwork for LLCs 
		3. Paperwork for C Corps (clerky clone?)
	3. main issues / obstacles to overcome:
		1. lot of touchpoints to formation related legal paperwork
		2. lot of touchpoint to archaic state filing systems
		3. would need to think about getting registered agent deals
	4. potential solution:
		1. start with a very small subset (core)
		2. outsource the formation to another company
2. EIN obtaining: fax etc
3. Financial operations: banking + card + savings + investments
	1. should be able to get a bank account once have EIN 
4. Company operations: Invoicing--send and receive money
5. Mailbox / lease (receiving mails)
6. Compliance: bookkeeping
7. Compliance: tax filing
8. Compliance: yearly tax filings 


2. EIN Obtaining
	1. Decision points: Apply online or via fax?
		1. Online application is faster and more convenient, but only available for businesses located in the U.S or its territories.
		2. Fax application is available for international applicants but may take up to four business days.
	2. Services
		1. Assistance in filling out SS-4 form.
		2. Guidance on how to apply online or via fax.
	3. Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
		1. Understanding the requirements and eligibility for obtaining an EIN.
		2. Navigating the IRS website or dealing with fax applications.
    4. Potential solution:
        1. Provide a step-by-step guide on how to obtain an EIN.
        2. Offer a service to handle the process on behalf of the client.

3. Financial Operations
    1.Decision points: Which bank? What type of account?
        1.Choosing a bank that offers business accounts with favorable terms and conditions.
        2.Deciding between a checking account, savings account, or both.
    2.Services
        1.Assistance in opening a bank account, including gathering necessary documents and completing application forms.
        2.Guidance on managing business finances, such as setting up automatic payments or transfers, using online banking services, etc.
    3.Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
        1.Understanding banking terms and conditions.
        2.Managing business finances effectively.

4.Company Operations
    1.Decision points: Which invoicing system? How to manage cash flow?
        1.Choosing an invoicing system that suits the company's needs and budget.
        2.Learning how to manage cash flow effectively to ensure financial stability of the company.
    2.Services
        1.Setting up an invoicing system and training staff on how to use it.
        2.Providing advice on cash flow management, such as setting payment terms, chasing overdue payments, etc.
    3.Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
        1.Finding a reliable and easy-to-use invoicing system.
        2.Managing cash flow effectively to avoid financial difficulties.

5.Mailbox/Lease
    1.Decision points: Physical or virtual mailbox? Long-term or short-term lease?
        1.Choosing between a physical mailbox (for receiving physical mail) and a virtual mailbox (for receiving digital mail).
        2.Deciding on the length of the lease based on the company's needs and budget.
    2.Services
        1.Setting up a mailbox service and managing incoming mail.
        2.Assisting in negotiating lease terms and conditions.
    3.Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
        1.Finding a reliable mailbox service that suits the company's needs.
        2.Negotiating favorable lease terms.

6. Compliance: Bookkeeping
	1. Decision points: In-house or outsourced bookkeeping? Which software to use?
		1. In-house bookkeeping gives more control but requires more resources, while outsourcing can be cost-effective but may result in less control.
		2. Choosing bookkeeping software that suits the company's needs and budget.
	2. Services
		1. Providing bookkeeping services, including recording transactions, reconciling accounts, preparing financial statements, etc.
		2. Assisting in setting up and using bookkeeping software.
	3. Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
		1. Keeping accurate and up-to-date financial records.
		2. Understanding bookkeeping principles and practices.

7.Compliance: Tax Filing
    1.Decision points: DIY or hire a professional? Which tax software to use?
        1.Filing taxes oneself can save money but may be time-consuming and complex, while hiring a professional can be costly but ensures accuracy and compliance.
        2.Choosing tax software that suits the company's needs and budget.
    2.Services
        1.Providing tax filing services, including preparing and submitting tax returns, claiming deductions, etc.
        2.Assisting in setting up and using tax software.
    3.Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
        1.Understanding tax laws and regulations.
        2.Ensuring accurate and timely tax filing.

8.Compliance: Yearly Tax Filings
    1.Decision points: When to file? What documents are needed?
        1.Knowing the deadlines for yearly tax filings to avoid penalties.
        2.Gathering all necessary documents for filing, such as income statements, expense receipts, etc.
    2.Services
        1.Reminding clients of upcoming tax deadlines and assisting in preparing for them.
        2.Helping clients gather necessary documents for yearly tax filings.
    3.Main issues/obstacles to overcome:
        1.Remembering all important tax deadlines.
        2.Preparing all necessary documents in time for yearly filings.
